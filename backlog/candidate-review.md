# Candidate Review

Status: Review record  
Date: 2026-07-07  
Source candidate pool: `research/backlog.md`  

This review checks the generated candidate agents against the foundation documents. It is not the approved execution sequence. `backlog/master-backlog.md` remains the canonical approved backlog.

## Review Criteria

Candidates were prioritised when they:

- start from a clear workflow, user, baseline and narrow problem;
- test one explicit hypothesis;
- can be built, evaluated and published within the daily rhythm;
- produce evidence for adoption, autonomy, cost, safety or engineering patterns;
- use synthetic, public or redacted data without avoidable risk;
- prefer the standard stack unless a variation is part of the hypothesis;
- improve the programme operating system, Agent Kit, templates, evaluation practice or Field Notes.

Candidates were moved down when they:

- depend on sensitive data, external communications or high-permission integrations too early;
- are too broad for one daily experiment;
- duplicate evidence likely to come from stronger candidates;
- risk unsupported claims, false assurance or hidden judgement;
- would be better as a later private case study after approval, permission and evaluation patterns are tested.

| 8 | Scope Change Assessor | Tests decision preparation, cost/value reasoning and autonomy limits in a practical delivery workflow. The agent recommends; humans keep commercial judgement. |
| 9 | Accessibility QA Agent | Good hybrid of deterministic tooling and AI interpretation. Tests where AI adds useful context to tool output rather than replacing existing checks. |
| 10 | Sensitive Data Redactor | Brings safety, publication constraints and redaction quality into the first third of the programme. High risk, but valuable early because many later private case studies depend on this discipline. |

## Rejected / Parked

Parked candidates are not bad ideas. They should not be treated as active replacements for the first 30 unless the scope is narrowed or evidence creates a specific reason to revive them.

| Candidate | Decision | Why |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Bid Response Assembler | Parked | Missing from the generated first/provisional/reserve grouping. The workflow is valuable, but it is broad, confidentiality-heavy and likely to pull in retrieval, approved source libraries, stale claims, commercial judgement and publication risk at once. It duplicates evidence likely to come from Case Study Drafting Assistant, Research Pack Builder, Proposal Risk Reviewer and Source Citation Checker. Revisit only as a narrow experiment, such as "flag unanswered RFP questions against an approved source pack." |

## Coverage Notes

- The first 10 intentionally include design discovery, structured extraction, review/risk, intake, content, trust, delivery planning, accessibility and safety. That avoids starting with one workflow or one demo format.
- The provisional 20 preserve coverage across all six research questions, especially autonomy, permission boundaries, structured outputs, cost and source-backed synthesis.
- The reserve pool should be used actively during weekly calibration. Swap from reserve when evidence shows duplication, a research gap or an unsustainable daily scope.
- Do not promote any candidate into `backlog/master-backlog.md` solely because it looks like a better demo. The foundation docs allow backlog changes for evidence, research gaps, better questions or sustainability.
